## 🚀 Introduction
This documentation provides a structured approach to performing OSINT (Open Source Intelligence) on phone numbers. Our objective is to assist researchers, security professionals, and students in verifying identities while maintaining high ethical standards.

## 🛡️ Prerequisites
To ensure your security during investigations, please adhere to these requirements:
* **Browser:** Use privacy-hardened browsers (e.g., **LibreWolf**, **Tor**).
* **Network:** Avoid tracking; maintain anonymity through secure network practices.
* **Environment:** Ensure your research environment is isolated and secure.

## 🔍 Investigation Methodology
1. **Search Engine Reconnaissance:** Deploy advanced Google Dorks and Boolean operators to uncover public digital footprints.
2. **Social Media Mapping:** Identify associated profiles on messaging platforms (WhatsApp, Telegram, Signal).
3. **Data Breach Analysis:** Cross-reference identifiers with authorized, public-domain breach databases (for research purposes only).
4. **Metadata Forensics:** If assets (images/files) are discovered, extract and analyze EXIF data to gather contextual intelligence.

## ⚖️ Ethical Guidelines
* **Non-Malicious Intent:** This repository is strictly for academic and security research.
* **Privacy Compliance:** Respect individual privacy; never engage in stalking, harassment, or unauthorized data collection.
* **Legal Compliance:** Ensure all research adheres to local and international privacy laws.
